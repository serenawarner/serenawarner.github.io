My work focuses on leveraging computational methods to better understand human behavior, cognition, and social understanding. I’m especially drawn to projects that use data to support self-reflection, identity exploration, and interpersonal understanding.

For my Senior Independent Study, I analyze large-scale personality trait data to construct theory-informed cognitive variables. I then apply Principal Component Analysis (PCA) and simulation-based validation to examine whether these variables reflect fewer underlying dimensions and what those dimensions may represent. This work explores how interpretable machine learning and statistical modeling can be used to revisit and evaluate classic psychological frameworks. I will present this work at Wooster’s I.S. Symposium and as a poster at NCUR (April 2026).

Outside of research, I’m deeply involved in STEM education and mentorship. As a STEM Success Initiative (SSI) Intern, I help plan and promote programming that supports confidence and belonging for underrepresented students in STEM. I also work as a STEM Zone Intern and Teaching Apprentice, supporting students through labs, office hours, and project-based courses in computer science.

Beyond coursework and research, I serve as Vice President of the Google Developer Student Club at Wooster. In my free time, I enjoy video games, journaling, and watching various TV shows.

I love research, teaching, and mentorship, and I’m excited by roles that allow me to build interpretable, human-centered models. I’m particularly interested in work at the intersection of behavioral data, cognition, and interpretability — whether in research or applied settings.

“I want to build systems that help people recognize themselves more clearly — not flatten them into categories, but give them something to hold onto in the fog.”

Thanks for getting to know me! Feel free to explore my projects or reach out.
